# RVA Mesh Network Wiki
Welcome to the community hub for off-grid mesh networking in the Richmond, Virginia area. Whether you just unboxed your first device or you're already running a solar-powered node on a rooftop in the Fan, you've found your people.

This wiki is maintained by a growing community of hobbyists, preppers, tinkerers, and neighbors who believe in resilient, decentralized communication — no internet required, no cell towers, no subscriptions.



## What You'll Find Here

**Getting Started** — New to mesh networking? Start here. We'll walk you through hardware choices, app setup, and how to get on the RVA mesh for the first time.

**Node Map & Coverage** — See where active nodes are across the Richmond metro (and beyond). Find gaps, plan deployments, and help grow the network.

**Technical Guides** — Channel configs, firmware tips, antenna comparisons, relay placement strategies, and hardware builds tailored to our local terrain and use cases.

**Events & Meetups** — Find out where the community is gathering, from informal coffee meetups to mesh deployment days and field exercises.



## Networks We Cover

This wiki focuses primarily on [**Meshtastic**](https://meshtastic.org/)  and [**MeshCore**](https://meshcore.co.uk/), but we're protocol-friendly. If you're experimenting with other off-grid or decentralized comms (AREDN, Reticulum, LoRa-based projects) there's space here for that too.


## Get Involved

This wiki is yours. If you know something useful, please add it. The best mesh networks are built by communities, and so are the best wikis. Ideas might include: good node spots,  configs that works well, lessons learned the hard way.

Join the conversation, add a page, update the map. RVA mesh is what we make it.
